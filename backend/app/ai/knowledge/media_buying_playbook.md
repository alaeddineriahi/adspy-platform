# Media-Buying Playbook (MENA e-commerce / COD) — reference knowledge

This is your working knowledge as a senior direct-response media buyer. Apply it;
don't recite it. Numbers are TYPICAL MENA ranges to anchor judgment, not
guarantees — always caveat that real numbers vary by product, offer and creative.

## 1. Core philosophy
- You don't "find the perfect campaign." You test cheaply, kill losers fast, and
  pour budget into the few winners. ~70-90% of tests fail; that's normal.
- Creative is 70%+ of performance. Targeting/structure is secondary now that Meta's
  algorithm is strong. A great creative saves a mediocre setup; a great setup can't
  save a weak creative.
- The product/offer matters more than the ads. A saturated or low-margin product
  can't be saved by media buying. Margin must cover COD returns + ad cost.
- Data > opinion. Never judge an ad set before it has meaningful data (spend ≈
  1-3× target CPA, or ~1,000+ reach / 3+ days). Don't turn things off at 2 hours.

## 2. Account structure & testing
- Beginner default on Meta: **ABO for testing, CBO for scaling** (ABO = you set
  budget per ad set so each creative gets a fair test; CBO/Advantage+ = Meta
  splits one budget across ad sets, better once you know your winners).
- Testing creatives: put 3-5 creatives in ONE broad ad set and let Meta find the
  winner, OR one creative per ad set if budget allows a fair test each. On small
  budgets, ONE ad set with your best 2-4 creatives beats many starved ad sets.
- Advantage+ Shopping (ASC): great default for e-com once you have a few solid
  creatives; it's broad + automated. Good for beginners who'd otherwise
  over-segment.
- Don't restructure daily. Give changes 2-3 days. Every edit resets the learning
  phase (the ~50-conversion period Meta needs to stabilize delivery).

## 3. Budgeting & the learning phase
- An ad set needs roughly **2-3× the product's target CPA per day** to exit the
  learning phase in a reasonable time. If target CPA is 10 and you feed 5/day, it
  may never stabilize.
- Budget → number of ad sets: small budget = FEWER ad sets. Rule of thumb:
  daily budget ÷ (2× target CPA) ≈ how many ad sets you can properly feed. Often
  that's 1 for a beginner.
- Scale budget in steps, not jumps. +20-30% every 2-3 profitable days on winners.
  Big jumps re-trigger learning and can tank performance.
- Set realistic test spend: budget to get ~3-5× target CPA of spend per creative
  before deciding. If you can't afford to test properly, test fewer things.

## 4. Audiences (broad-first era)
- Start BROAD (no interests, just country + age/gender if the product demands it).
  Meta's algorithm targets off the creative + pixel better than manual interests
  for most products now.
- Interests: use only to seed a niche the algo can't infer, or to test angles.
  Keep them big (>1-2M). Stacking many tiny interests hurts.
- Lookalikes: only once you have enough pixel data (≥100 purchases ideally). 1-3%
  LLA of purchasers is strongest. Beginners usually don't have the data yet — start
  broad instead.
- Age/gender: narrow only when the product is obviously gendered/aged. Otherwise let
  it run broad and read the breakdowns after data comes in.

## 5. Creatives
- Formats: short-form UGC video (best for cold, especially TikTok & Reels),
  problem/solution demos, before/after, and simple bold-text images/carousels for
  offers. Video usually wins cold traffic; images can win for clear offers.
- Hooks (first 3s) decide everything. Types: problem/pain, curiosity, bold claim,
  social proof, contrarian. Test multiple hooks on the SAME body.
- How many to start: aim for 3-5 distinct creative CONCEPTS (not 5 tweaks of one).
  With only 1-2, that's enough to start but tell them to build more — winners come
  from volume of angles.
- Iterate winners: when one works, spin variations (new hooks, new intros, new
  proof) rather than starting from scratch. Creative fatigue (rising CPM / falling
  CTR over days) means refresh the creative, not the audience.
- Cheap creative for beginners: film UGC on a phone, use the product in-hand,
  customer testimonials, or repurpose the ANGLE of a proven ad from AdSpy (never
  copy assets 1:1).

## 6. KPIs, funnel & rough MENA ranges
Read the funnel top-to-bottom to locate the leak:
- **CPM** (cost per 1,000 impressions): MENA is cheap — often ~2-8 USD equivalent;
  Gulf (SA/AE) higher than North Africa. High CPM = broaden audience / better
  placements / creative flagged.
- **CTR (link)**: aim >1%. <1% cold = weak hook/creative or wrong audience.
- **Hook rate** (3s video views ÷ impressions): >25-30% is healthy; low = weak first 3s.
- **CPC (link)**: derived from CPM & CTR; high CPC almost always = low CTR = creative.
- **ATC → Purchase**: big drop = landing page, price, trust, or offer problem, not ads.
- **CPA / CPP** (cost per purchase): must be < (margin after COD returns). This is
  the number that decides profit.
- **ROAS**: for COD, "breakeven ROAS" must also absorb the unconfirmed/returned
  orders — see §8. A 2.0 ROAS can still lose money at 50% delivery.

## 7. Diagnosis playbook (symptom → likely cause → fix)
- Low CTR, low CPM → creative/hook weak → new hooks & angles; broaden.
- Low CTR, high CPM → audience too small or creative flagged → broaden; check policy.
- Good CTR, few ATC → landing page / price / product-market fit → fix LP, price, offer.
- Good ATC, few purchases → checkout friction / trust / COD hesitation → simplify
  form (name+phone only for COD), add reviews, reassure, offer COD clearly.
- Good metrics, still unprofitable → margin too thin or COD returns too high → raise
  price/AOV (bundles, upsells), improve confirmation & delivery.
- Great for 3 days then dying → creative fatigue → refresh creative; don't just
  raise budget.
- Volatile / never stabilizes → too many ad sets / budget too low / editing too
  often → consolidate, feed properly, stop fiddling.

## 8. COD economics (critical in MENA)
- COD orders are NOT confirmed sales. Typical **confirmation** ~50-75% (call center
  reaches & confirms), then **delivery/acceptance** ~60-85% of confirmed. Net
  delivered can be ~40-65% of "orders."
- So an order at 8 CPA with 55% net delivery really costs ~14.5 per delivered order.
  Your product margin must beat the DELIVERED cost, not the raw CPA.
- Levers: strong call-center follow-up (fast confirmation), clear COD messaging,
  realistic delivery times, and raising AOV with bundles/upsells so one delivery
  carries more margin.
- Prepaid (card) where it exists (Gulf) has better economics — push it when the
  market supports it; North Africa is COD-dominant.

## 9. Country notes
- **Tunisia (TND)**: COD-dominant, dropship-heavy, French/darija creative, cheap
  CPMs, Converty/YouCan stores common. Meta strong; TikTok rising.
- **Morocco (MAD)**: COD, darija/French, similar to TN, slightly bigger market.
- **Algeria (DZD)**: large market, COD, Arabic/darija, delivery logistics harder,
  confirmation quality matters a lot.
- **Egypt (EGP)**: huge & cheap CPMs, Arabic, price-sensitive, returns can be high;
  volume game.
- **Saudi (SAR) / UAE (AED)**: higher AOV & CPMs, more prepaid, Arabic + English,
  premium positioning works; better margins, more competition.
- Match language to market (Arabic / French / darija) and platform to audience
  (TikTok skews younger; Meta broader).

## 10. Common beginner mistakes to call out
- Budget spread across too many ad sets → none exit learning.
- Killing ads in hours / editing constantly → resets learning, no clean data.
- Only 1 creative, or 5 tweaks of the same idea → no real test of angles.
- Chasing ROAS while ignoring COD delivery → "profitable" campaigns that lose cash.
- Low-margin or saturated product → no media buying fixes bad unit economics.
- Blaming targeting when the creative or the offer is the real problem.
- Scaling too fast / big budget jumps → tanks a working ad set.
